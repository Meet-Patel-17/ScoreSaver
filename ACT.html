ACT Test Structure

The ACT is a 2-hour 55-minute exam (3 hours 30 minutes including breaks) with four sections: English, math, reading, and science. The scoring ranges from 1 to 36 with no penalty for wrong answers.

ACT Sections and Timing

  • English - 45 minutes, 75 questions
  • Math - 60 minutes, 60 questions
  • Reading - 35 minutes, 40 questions
  • Science - 35 minutes, 40 questions

How to Study for ACT?

I would recommend doing practice exams from past ACTs. Make sure to do each section in one sitting and then score yourself. For the questions you got wrong, make sure you understand why that happened. Additionally, act.org offers free practice resources. When you take practice tests, simulate the environment as if it were a real test!

Why is ACT Important?

If you aim for top colleges, you might want to get a good ACT score, preferably in the 34-36 range. This can significantly boost your chances during the college application process.
