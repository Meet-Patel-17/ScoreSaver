SAT Test Structure

The SAT is a little different this year. The new SAT format is shorter at 2 hours and 15 minutes, whereas the old exam is 3 hours and 15 minutes. The old SAT included three sections, reading, writing, and math. The digital SAT format only includes two, reading and writing, and math.

SAT Sections and Timing

  • Math - 70 minutes calculator with ALL questions
  • Reading and Writing - Combined into 1 section with 54 multiple choice questions

How to Study for SAT?

Taking practice tests, knowing the format, and using Khan Academy as a resource for the digital SAT is a great way to study for the test. You will find all the links below so you don't have to research the best sites to study for the SAT.

Why SAT is Important?

If you are someone who would like to go to top colleges, you might want to get a good SAT score, meaning a score like 1500-1600. This is why you should study for it to boost your chances in your college application. If not, you can still use it to get a scholarship that would save you money for college.
